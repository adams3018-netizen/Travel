Prague

Prague rises from both banks of the Vltava like a city preserved inside a storybook. Gothic towers, Baroque façades, quiet courtyards, and cobbled lanes unfold around the river, while the Charles Bridge carries visitors between the medieval Old Town and the castle district above. Known as the City of a Hundred Spires, Prague is a place where centuries of imperial history, café culture, music, and late-night adventure seem to coexist on the same street.

Munich

Munich wears its history lightly. Royal avenues and Gothic towers open onto lively squares, while cyclists disappear into the vast English Garden and long communal tables fill beneath chestnut trees. In summer, the city stretches the evening along the Isar with outdoor bars, music and theatre. It is unmistakably Bavarian—beer halls, brass bands and pretzels—but also polished, playful and surprisingly international once the sun goes down.

Paris

Paris has a way of making arrivals feel cinematic. Grand boulevards open toward monuments, the Seine divides and reconnects the city, and every neighborhood seems to possess its own rhythm. On Sundays, cafés spill onto pavements, gardens fill with walkers, and the city feels less like a museum than a stage still in use. For this journey, Paris is not simply the final stop. It is the destination the entire route has been moving toward.
